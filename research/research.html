Matti Ryynänen

Department of Signal Processing, Tampere University of Technology

Worked in the Audio Research Group as a researcher and as a post-graduate student March 2002 - January 2009, obtaining Dr. Tech. in January 2009. My research focused on automatic music-content analysis and, especially, on the automatic transcription of music and its applications. This work was funded by Tampere Graduate School in Information Science and Engineering.

For current activities, see here.

Research

Automatic transcription of polyphonic music

  • Automatic transcription of melody, bass line, and chords in polyphonic music (added 2008). Example page.

  • Automatic bass line transcription from streaming polyphonic audio (added 9/2006).

  • Automatic transcription of the singing melody in polyphonic music (added 4/2006).

  • Polyphonic music transcription using note event modeling (added 4/2005). Example page includes transcription examples from real-world music, produced by a polyphonic music transcription method.

  • Monophonic singing transcription (added 5/2004). Example page contains demonstrations of automatic singing transcription, including input and output sound files.

Applications based on automatic music transcription

  • Accompaniment separation and karaoke application based on automatic melody transcription (added 3/2008). Example page.

  • Query by humming (QBH) of MIDI and audio (added 12/2007). Example page.
